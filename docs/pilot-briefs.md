# Pilot Briefs

One-page briefs for the three target verticals. Each pilot runs on the live demo
(no credentials) and can point at the customer's environment via the Integration
Contract for a production pilot.

**Live demo:** https://codexdominion-command-console.vercel.app

---

## 🏛 Government — AI Governance for Contracts & Procurement

**Problem.** Agencies must prove that automated decisions, contractor payments,
and AI systems are governed, reviewable, and auditable — for FedRAMP, NIST AI
RMF, and oversight.

**What CodexDominion shows.**
- **Command Workspace:** "Prepare for a FedRAMP assessment" → a governed,
  human-approved execution plan grounded in *your* posture.
- **GCFI module:** contract milestones, contractor approvals, and **payment
  authorization risk** (e.g., missing dual approval) governed and audited.
- **Procurement:** opportunities matched to required controls + capability gaps.
- **Audit trail:** every action hash-chained and examiner-ready.

**Pilot scope (30–60 days).** Bind one agency program's data via the contract;
demonstrate governed decisions, evidence packs, and a FedRAMP readiness plan.

**Proof points.** Knowledge graph of the environment · tamper-evident audit ·
role-controlled authority · deterministic, explainable execution.

## 🏥 Healthcare — Clinical AI Accountability

**Problem.** Health systems deploying clinical AI must enforce HIPAA, minimize
PHI, and produce audit evidence for every automated decision.

**What CodexDominion shows.**
- **Command Workspace:** "Prepare for a HIPAA audit" → governed multi-step plan
  (risk posture → ComplianceFlow → vendor certs → evidence) with approval.
- **ComplianceFlow module:** SOC 2 / control evidence governed live-or-demo.
- **Decisions:** PHI-minimization and consent rules enforced, flagged, audited.
- **Vendor governance:** HIPAA / SOC 2 / insurance status with expiry warnings.

**Pilot scope (30–60 days).** Govern one clinical AI workflow end-to-end:
decision logging, evidence generation, and an audit-ready HIPAA pack.

**Proof points.** Governed knowledge of vendors/policies/decisions · evidence on
demand · RBAC (clinician vs. compliance vs. auditor).

## 💳 Finance — Model Risk & Fair-Lending Governance

**Problem.** Financial institutions must govern AI lending/fraud models under
SR 11-7 and fair-lending rules, with reviewer sign-off and defensible evidence.

**What CodexDominion shows.**
- **Decisions:** model-risk and disparate-impact rules enforced; approve/deny
  with reviewer attribution and cryptographic evidence hashes.
- **Command Workspace:** "Review system risk posture" / "Review AI governance
  posture" → governed, context-aware results.
- **Evidence packs:** examination-ready bundles for denied decisions.
- **codex-control-plane module:** the governance engine, live-bindable.

**Pilot scope (30–60 days).** Govern one lending/fraud model: decision capture,
fair-lending checks, reviewer workflow, and a quarterly evidence pack.

**Proof points.** SR 11-7 / fair-lending mapping · hash-chained audit · governed
knowledge graph · live module binding path.

for **Caribbean** markets, **Canadian** healthcare, and **multinational**

## Common pilot motion

1. **Demo** on the live URL (no setup) using `docs/demo-script.md`.
2. **Scope** one workflow/module for a 30–60 day paid pilot.
3. **Bind** the customer's data via the Integration Contract (demo → live).
4. **Prove** governance: decisions, evidence, audit, role control — and an
   execution plan tailored to the vertical.
